Child Safety & CSAE Prevention Standards — BongDate

Last updated: 10 December 2025 · Publicly published child safety standards and reporting contact

Overview

BongDate is committed to protecting children and preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE), including child sexual abuse material (CSAM), in our app and services. This page describes our public safety standards, technical and operational measures, reporting mechanisms, and contact information for CSAE/CSAM concerns.

Safety standards URL: https://www.bongdate.app/child-safety-policy.html

Important: This page is publicly accessible and describes our policies and contact point for CSAM prevention and response.

Scope & Definitions

Scope: Applies to the BongDate mobile app (Android / iOS), website properties under bongdate.app, and services provided by BongDate.

Definitions:

  • CSAE: Child sexual abuse and exploitation — any sexual activity or exploitation involving persons under 18 years of age.
  • CSAM: Child sexual abuse material — images, videos or other media depicting sexual abuse or exploitation of a minor.
  • User-generated content (UGC): Content uploaded, posted, or transmitted by users including images, videos, chat messages and profiles.

Prevention & Safety Measures

We use a layered approach combining prevention, detection, reporting, and remediation:

1. Age verification & account controls

  • Users must confirm their age during signup. We do not intentionally allow accounts for persons under 13. Accounts suspected to belong to minors are evaluated and removed where appropriate.
  • Where required by law, we implement stricter age-gating flows and parental consent mechanisms.

2. Content moderation & detection

  • Automated detection systems: hashing, image analysis, metadata checks and pattern detection are used to flag potential CSAM and abusive material.
  • Human review: flagged content is reviewed by trained moderators following strict privacy and safety procedures.
  • Proactive removal: confirmed CSAM or any sexual content involving minors is removed immediately and the account is disabled pending investigation.

3. Reporting & in-app safety features

  • In-app reporting tools allow users to report content, messages, profiles, or behavior suspected of CSAE. Reports can be submitted anonymously and are prioritized for review.
  • Users can block and mute other users and limit who can message them.

4. Technical measures

  • Secure transmission (HTTPS/TLS) and encrypted storage for user data where appropriate.
  • Rate-limiting and behavior monitoring to detect grooming or predatory patterns.
  • Use of industry-standard hash-matching (e.g., PhotoDNA or equivalent) to compare content against known CSAM databases, where permitted by law and platform rules.

5. Staff training & policies

  • Moderators and relevant staff receive specialized training on child safety, mandatory reporting obligations, and trauma-informed review practices.
  • Clear escalation procedures exist for urgent reports and suspected ongoing harm.

How to Report CSAM / CSAE

If you encounter content or behavior suggesting child sexual abuse or exploitation, please report it immediately using any of the following channels:

  1. In-app report: Tap the ••• / report button on the offending content or profile and choose the relevant reason (e.g., "sexually explicit content", "minor in sexual context", "grooming").
  2. Email our designated contact: For policy, compliance, and high-priority reports email business.hrithikadhikary@gmail.com. Include the app username, a description, links/screenshots, timestamps, and any relevant message IDs.
  3. Emergency / law enforcement: If a child is in immediate danger or you suspect ongoing abuse, contact your local law enforcement or emergency services first and then notify us.

When you report, we will:

  • Confirm receipt of your report (where contact details are provided).
  • Immediately triage and prioritize reports indicating imminent risk or confirmed CSAM.
  • Remove content that violates our policies and suspend or terminate offending accounts.
  • Preserve and secure evidence necessary for lawful disclosure to authorities and to comply with platform / legal requirements.

Note on anonymous reports: We accept anonymous reports but may need more information to investigate thoroughly. If you can safely provide contact information we can follow up.

Cooperation with Law Enforcement & Trusted Agencies

BongDate cooperates with law enforcement, national hotlines, and trusted non-governmental organizations as required by law to prevent, investigate, and respond to CSAE and CSAM. We will disclose information (subject to legal process) necessary for criminal investigations and victim protection.

Retention & evidence preservation: Relevant records (account information, IP logs, message metadata, media files) may be preserved and disclosed to authorities in accordance with law.

Privacy & Data Handling

We balance safety investigations with privacy obligations. All investigations are handled on a need-to-know basis. Data shared with third parties or law enforcement follows legal requirements and our published Privacy Policy.

For details on data retention, user rights, and privacy practices, please see our full Privacy Policy: https://www.bongdate.app/privacy-policy.html

Transparency, Reporting & Updates

We publish aggregated transparency reports summarizing the volume of reports, content removals, and actions taken related to CSAE/CSAM on an annual or periodic basis.
